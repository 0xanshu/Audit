# Go-To-Market Strategy

## Target Customer

**Primary:** Engineering leads and CTOs at Series A–C startups (20–200 employees) who have adopted multiple AI tools in the last 18 months and have no systematic way to audit what they're paying for.

**Profile:**

- Title: VP Engineering, CTO, Head of Engineering, or Technical Co-founder
- Company: 20–200 employees, $2M–$50M ARR, Series A–C
- Pain: They approved 5–10 AI tool subscriptions over the last year, each individually reasonable, but nobody has looked at the total bill or whether the tools overlap
- Budget authority: Yes, or direct access to the person who has it
- Discovery channel: Twitter/X, private Slack communities (Rands Leadership Slack, CTO Craft), LinkedIn

**Secondary:** Finance/ops leads at the same companies who are doing a quarterly software spend review and need a fast way to audit the AI line items.

---

## First 100 Users

**Week 1–2: Direct outreach in communities**

Post a genuine, non-spammy message in 3–5 private communities:

- Rands Leadership Slack (#tools, #engineering-management)
- CTO Craft Slack
- Indie Hackers
- Hacker News "Show HN" post

Message framing: "I built a free tool that audits your AI tool spend in 60 seconds. No login required. Would love feedback from anyone managing a team with multiple AI subscriptions."

Target: 20–30 users from community posts.

**Week 3–4: Twitter/X distribution**

Post a thread: "We analyzed 50 AI tool stacks. Here's what we found:" with anonymized aggregate data from early audits (average overspend, most common duplicate tools, most over-provisioned tool). End with a link to run your own audit.

Target: 30–50 users from organic Twitter reach.

**Week 5–6: LinkedIn targeting**

Write a post aimed at CTOs: "Your team is probably paying for 3 tools that do the same thing. Here's how to find out in 60 seconds." Direct link to the tool.

Target: 20–30 users from LinkedIn.

**Ongoing: Viral loop**

Every audit report has an Open Graph image that says "This team could save $X/month on AI tools." When someone shares their report link in Slack or email, the preview card does the marketing. No additional effort required.

---

## Conversion Funnel

```
Visitor → Runs audit (no login) → Sees savings → Submits email → Creates account → Shares report
  100%         ~60%                   ~60%            ~20%            ~10%             ~5%
```

At 100 visitors: ~60 audits run, ~12 emails captured, ~6 accounts created, ~3 report shares.

Each share drives ~5 new visitors (Slack/email forwarding), creating a compounding loop.
