# Go-To-Market

## Target User

VP Eng / CTO / Head of Engineering at a Series A–C startup (20–200 people). They've approved 5–10 AI tool subscriptions over the past year, each individually reasonable, but nobody's looked at the combined bill or checked for overlap. They have budget authority.

**Secondary:** Finance/ops leads at the same companies doing quarterly software spend reviews who need a fast way to audit the AI line items.

## What They're Doing Right Before They'd Want This

Staring at a line item in their monthly software bill that says "AI Tools — $4,200" and thinking "that can't be right." Or getting a Slack DM from their CFO asking "can you break down the AI spend?"

## Where They Hang Out

- Rands Leadership Slack (#tools, #engineering-management)
- CTO Craft Slack
- Indie Hackers
- Twitter/X — following @swyx, @GergelyOrosz, AI-tools-discussion accounts
- Hacker News

## First 100 Users ($0 Budget)

**Week 1–2:** Post in 3–5 private Slack communities. Not a pitch — just: "I built a free tool that audits your AI spend in 60 seconds. No login. Would love feedback." Target: 20–30 users.

**Week 3–4:** Twitter thread: "We analyzed 50 AI tool stacks. Here's what we found" — anonymized aggregate data from early audits (avg overspend, most common duplicates, most over-provisioned tool). Link at the end. Target: 30–50 users.

**Week 5–6:** LinkedIn post aimed at CTOs: "Your team is probably paying for 3 tools that do the same thing." Direct link. Target: 20–30 users.

## Unfair Distribution Channel

Every audit report has an OG image that says "This team could save $X/month on AI tools." When someone drops the link in Slack or emails it to their CFO, the preview card does the marketing. Zero effort virality.

## Week-1 Traction (If This Works)

50+ audits completed, 10+ emails captured, 3+ shared report links generating inbound traffic. Average savings identified > $400/mo — proof the tool is reaching the right audience.

## Conversion Funnel

```
Visitor → Runs audit → Sees savings → Submits email → Creates account → Shares report
  100%       ~60%         ~60%           ~20%            ~10%              ~5%
```

Each share drives ~5 new visitors via Slack/email forwarding → compounding loop.
